Chapter One
A Fun Day at the Park

It was a fun day at the Grove Miller Ball Park!

The sun was very bright and everyone was happy.

Today was the long awaited Championship Game.

The Blue Jays and the Red Birds were the two best baseball teams in town.

All the fans were cheering for their two favorite teams.

The Blue Jays were winning over the Red Birds. The Blue Jay's score was four. The Red Birds' score was three.

Dan and Tap played on the Blue Jay's team. Sam played for the Red Birds' team.

The baseball game was almost over. It was the bottom of the Ninth Inning.

The Red Birds had two outs with one player on third base.

"We must get the next player out," said Dan on the Blue Jay's team.

1
Chapter Two
Sam the Next Batter

It was Sam's time to hit for the Red Birds. He had the bat in his hands.

Dan and Tap looked at each other and smiled. They were happy!

"Could this be a sure win for our team?" said Tap.

"Maybe," said Dan. "Sam has not hit a ball all year."

The Blue Jays' pitcher threw one strike ball, Sam did not swing. The Blue Jays' pitcher threw a second strike ball, Sam did not swing. The Blue Jays' pitcher threw one ball, Sam did not swing. Sam now had two strikes and one ball.

Dan and Tap smiled at each other when Sam got two strikes.

"One more strike and the game will be ours!" said Dan to Tap!

The Red Birds' team and the fans were cheering Sam on. You can do it Sam!

Sam was sad. He did not want to let his team down. Then he said, "I can hit the ball! I will hit the ball! I will not strike out!"

For once, Sam believed in himself. The ball was coming fast.

2
Chapter Three
All Eyes Are Now on Tap

Sam kept his eyes on the ball. Then he swung his bat. The ball went high into the sky.

Sam's heart was beating fast. The team yelled, "Run Sam and get on base!"

The ball was still going up and out. Sam threw his bat down and began to run. The player on third base ran to home plate to score. The game is now tied four to four.

The Red Birds' team and fans were jumping and cheering. The Blue Jays' team and fans were saying, "Oh no, this is not good!"

Then all eyes turned to right field where Tap was running to catch the ball.

Dan turned and yelled to his friend. "The ball is coming your way Tap. Run faster Tap, you must catch that ball!"

Tap was also running fast in right field. "Oh my," said Tap, "the sun's rays are bright. I cannot see the ball for the bright sun light."

Tap tried to block the bright sun light with his glove.

Dan said to himself, "I have never seen Tap run so fast! I know he will catch this ball. Tap has not missed catching a ball all year."

3
Chapter Four
What Happens Next?

All Dan could think about now is when the game is over. The Blue Jays will go out for pizza to celebrate their Championship Game win.

Just for a few seconds Dan closed his eyes. "Yummy, yummy! I can smell and taste the cheese and pepperoni pizza. It will be hot and oh so good!"

When Dan opened his eyes, he said "Oh no! What is that? Could that be? Oh my, look at Pat our team's mascot The Fat Cat. She is running onto the baseball field!"

Pat, The Fat Cat, saw Tap and wants to play. Pat ran to the right field where Tap was running to catch the ball.

Some of the fans were laughing to see Pat the mascot on the field. The Blue Jays coaches were yelling for Pat, The Fat Cat to get off the field.

What will The Fat Cat do? Will Tap catch the ball? Who will win the Championship game?

4
Chapter Five
Who Wins the Game

Tap was proud that he had run fast enough to catch the ball. The sun was no longer in his eyes. Tap kept his eyes focused on the ball as it came down. He reached out his glove to follow the ball down, down, down.

Tap said with confidence, "I will catch the ball!" His heart was beating fast from running so far.

Suddenly Tap heard the familiar voice of his dear friend. Dan yelled, "Tap watch out!" Tap took his eyes off the ball for a second. He glanced down just in time to see Pat, The Fat Cat on the field. He stopped just short of running over her.

Pat sat right in front of Tap, looked up at him and said, "Meow, meow."

Because of the Blue Jays mascot Pat, The Fat Cat, Tap had to jump over her. When Tap found the ball again, it was so low that he had to dive on the ground.

As Tap slid to catch the ball with his glove stretched out, the ball came down. Just as Tap stopped sliding, the ball hit the tip of the glove, and rolled onto the ground.

The game was over as Sam ran home and scored from his first hit of the year. The Red Birds were the new champions. The score was Red Birds five and the Blue Jays four.

5
Chapter Six
The Real Celebration

The real celebration came when Sam took a few seconds to believe in himself. He said, "I can hit the ball. I will hit the ball. I will not strike out."

Sam felt a sense of pride as he ran across home plate for the winning score. Sam did not let his team down. The Red Birds were the new champions for the town.

Sam's mother, father, and brother jumped and cheered. The team lifted Sam high into the air. The fans all ran onto the field. This was a great day for Sam and the Red Birds.

Sam's first hit helped him to believe in himself. He learned something valuable today.

Sam's Coach, Mr. Harris, congratulated him on his winning hit. Mr. Harris told Sam that he always knew that one day he would find that self confidence in himself if only he believed!

6
Chapter Seven
Tap Lay on the Ground

Tap, still stretched out on the ground could not believe just what had happened. He was very, very sad. It was the first ball he missed catching all year.

He looked around for Pat the team's mascot. He saw her running back under the stands.

By that time, Dan ran over to his friend. "Tap, are you okay?" Tap looked sadly at his best friend and said "I'm okay. I am not hurt, only sad."

Dan sat on the ground next to his friend and put his arm around him. They were both sad that their team lost the championship game.

"I wish that Pat, The Fat Cat had not run onto the field," said Dan. "You would have caught that ball Tap, I just know it!"

Then the two friends got off the ground and high-fived each other. Dan and Tap said, "We did not win the Championship Game, but we sure played our best!"

7
Chapter Eight
Good Sportsmanship

The game was over. All the boys and girls lined up. The two teams shook hands to show good sportsmanship. (Respect for each player)

The Red Birds won the Championship Game and the News traveled all over town. The Blue Jays were sad that they lost the game but happy for all their friends on the Red Bird's team.

All the boys and girls had lots of fun today playing in the Championship Game!

The Blue Jay's head coach let all the boys and girls know just how proud he was of the team. Coach Brown told the team that they played like champions and showed great sportsmanship.

He told the Blue Jays, "Sometimes how you lose is just as valuable as how you win."

Dan said "What do you mean Coach Brown?"

What I mean said Coach Brown. "If a team loses, after a fair and hard fought game, and each player did his or her very best, then every boy or girl should feel good. That is good sportsmanship! That makes me most proud of you as a team. Practice will start next spring. We will work harder and prepare better for next year's Championship Game."

8
Chapter Nine
Pizza Celebration

Sam's mother and father over heard what the Blue Jays said. Dan and Tap said that it is okay to feel sad that the Blue Jays lost. The Red Birds are still our friends. Yes said Tap. "I was happy to see Sam hit the ball. I know that made him feel so good. We must not feel sad about their win."

Sam's parents thought that the Blue Jays showed good sportsmanship today. So, Sam's parents decided to take both teams out for pizza. This was a very good way to celebrate a Championship Game.

All the parents and fans came along to celebrate the two best teams. They ate lots of pizza.

Dan said, "The cheese and pepperoni pizza is hot and oh so good! Yummmmm!"

What a great day! The Championship Game was over. The pizza celebration ended. Tap said to Dan, "The Red Birds will be the champions for one year."

Then Dan said to the Red Birds, "See you next year at the Championship Game!"

After a great day of baseball and good pizza, the two friends began to walk home. Tap said, "By the way Dan, should we leave Pat, The Fat Cat at home next year?" 😄
